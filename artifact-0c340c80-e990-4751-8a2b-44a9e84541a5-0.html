Lifting Work — §7.1
Crane operations, rigging, signal persons
9 rules▼
👷 Human
All personnel (crane operator, signal person, rigger, crane supervisor) must hold legally certified training and be registered with LSP HSSE sticker.
Mandatory
Blind lifting in open area: 1 signal person; near plant equipment/buildings: min. 2 signal persons + 2 flagmen at front/rear of crane.
📋 Method
Prepare a Lifting Plan (owner's form) before each lifting shift; survey and barricade the area, hang warning signs before commencing permit.
Mandatory
Do not lift objects with unknown weight/no calculation; lifting equipment must clearly display SWL/WLL.
No lifting during rain/storm; crane near high-voltage lines must maintain ≥5 m clearance; retract boom when not operating.
Mandatory
Night lifting must provide sufficient working illumination.
Caution
⚙️ Machinery
Cranes and lifting equipment must undergo annual 3rd-party certification AND LSP Maintenance inspection with sticker before use; daily inspection with records.
Required
High-risk cranes must notify BRVT DOLISA before bringing into LSP — per Article 16, Decree 44/2016/NDCP.
Legal
🧗
Working at Height — §7.4
Fall prevention, harness, safety net
10 rules▼
👷 Human
Mandatory full-body Safety Harness + Double Lanyard, 100% tie-off at heights. Anchor point load-rated ≥15 kN.
Mandatory
Supervisor/Safety Officer/Job Owner must closely monitor throughout the work. Observer stationed at blind spots.
📋 Method
Load-bearing material rated ≥120 kg/m² or install temporary guardrails.
Required
Safe distance from power lines: 2.4m (<12kV) / 3.0m (<33kV) / 3.3m (<69kV) / 3.9m (<115kV) / 5.3m (<230kV).
Required
📦 Material
Safety Net and Safety Life Line must be load tested at 2× person's weight before use.
Mandatory
🌿 Environment
Prohibited during thunderstorm, heavy rain, or insufficient lighting.
Mandatory
Barricade below: ≥2m (height <12m) / ≥3m (height ≥12m).
🚨 Emergency
Safety Net below work area required. Personnel below must wear hard hats. Work on pillars/tanks/stacks: mandatory guardrail + lifeline + harness.
Critical
🪜
Scaffolding & Ladders — §7.5 / §7.17
Erection, inspection, Civil Engineer certification
14 rules▼
👷 Human
Assembly/dismantling: only 3rd-party certified personnel. Women prohibited on scaffolding >10m.
Mandatory
Persons with hypertension, heart disease, epilepsy, or acrophobia are prohibited from working on scaffolding.
Banned
Supervisor + Safety Lead must remain on site continuously during assembly/dismantling.
Ladder: max 10 min continuous use; working point ≤5m; always 3-point contact when climbing/descending.
Caution
📋 Method
Work Permit + Risk Assessment before erection. Scaffold inspection every ≤7 days by competent person, or after any abnormal event.
Permit
Transfer scaffolding equipment hand-to-hand — NEVER stand on same vertical axis within falling-object zone.
Mandatory
Civil Engineer certification: 12m–21m → Associate; >21m → Professional Civil Engineer; load >200kg → Associate Civil Engineer.
Required
Clearance from power lines: ≥2.4m (50V–12kV) / ≥3.0m (12–33kV) / ≥3.9m (69–115kV).
Required
📦 Material
Scaffold material per BS 1139; solid wooden planks with toe board ≥10cm; guardrail: top 90–110cm, mid-rail 45–55cm.
Required
Color tag (Tagging) system — inspected and recorded at LSP Maintenance.
🚨 Emergency
Safety Net load test at 2× person's weight. Do NOT pull equipment along guardrail — will damage it.
Critical

MEWPs — Scissor / Boom Lift — §7.7
Mobile elevated work platforms
6 rules▼
👷 Human
MEWP operator must be trained and certified; mandatory Safety Harness + Double Lanyard when working at height.
Mandatory
📋 Method
Do not reposition/move to new work point without lowering platform to ground first; prohibited on slopes >30°.
Banned
Do not exceed SWL; NEVER climb outside the defensive line (guardrail).
Banned
⚙️ Machinery
MEWP must have audible and visual warning signals active at all times during operation.
Required

LOTO — Lock-out / Tag-out — §7.12
Energy isolation, personal red locks
8 rules▼
👷 Human
Each individual applies their own Personal Red Lock before working. Only the owner may remove it.
Mandatory
📋 Method
Work Permit required; shut down machine → cut main switch → hang "DO NOT OPERATE – Personnel Working" sign → apply LOTO per owner's procedure.
Permit
Remove hazardous parts first (pinch points, sharp points, gears); use only standard tools — no improvised/modified tools.
Required
Changes to the method vs. initial setup → re-evaluate JSA before continuing.
Caution
⚡
Electrical Work — §7.14
De-energize, LOTO, ELCB
8 rules▼
👷 Human
Electrical workers trained per Circular 05/2021/TT-BCT; minimum 2 persons when electrical contact risk is present.
Legal
📋 Method
De-energize before work (performed by owner); LOTO from ≥24V upward.
Permit
Confirm system is de-energized before starting; capacitors/transformers must be discharged prior to work.
Mandatory
PROHIBITED: modifying electrical equipment; plugging 220V/380V directly at production areas (except lighting and standard hand tools).
Banned
Newly installed electrical equipment: must install ELCB ≤30mA and be inspected/approved by LSP Electrical Dept (PSSR/Audit team).
Required
⚙️ Machinery
All electrical equipment certified by LSP Electrical Dept; grounding at designated owner points.
Required
🚨 Emergency
CO₂/dry powder extinguisher (NOT water) at electrical areas. All personnel know CPR and electrical shock first aid.
Critical
⚗️
Chemical Operations — §7.10
Storage, handling, GHS labeling
12 rules▼
👷 Human
Basic chemical safety training; 3rd-party training per Decree 113/2017/NĐ-CP.
Legal
No smoking, eating, drinking, or food storage in chemical areas. Drowsiness/breathing difficulties → evacuate immediately and report to Supervisor.
Banned
📋 Method
Submit SDS + chemical import permit (if applicable) to owner before introducing chemicals to the area.
Mandatory
Measure O₂ in poorly ventilated areas: 19.5%–23.5%. Chemical concentration must not exceed legal/international limits.
Required
Diluting acid: pour acid INTO water — NEVER the reverse. Do not mix reactive chemicals; keep ≥5m apart.
Mandatory
Use metal containers only + anti-static grounding (except chemicals reactive with metal). Plastic jerry cans PROHIBITED.
Banned
📦 Material
Full GHS label: product name, chemical name, hazard symbols, Signal Word, Hazard & Precautionary Statements.
Required
Metal containment (≥2.5mm) holding 100% of stored chemical volume at all times.
Required
🚨 Emergency
Emergency shower + eyewash station (fixed or portable) at work area; inform all personnel before starting. Chemical spill/fire → stop work, move to emergency assembly point; chemical injury → first aid immediately, notify Safety Officer.
Critical
🎨
Painting & Anti-rust — §7.19
Paints, solvents, fire prevention
7 rules▼
👷 Human
Wear chemical filter mask at all times when mixing and applying paint; appropriate chemical-resistant gloves.
Mandatory
📋 Method
Work Permit required; inspect work area — NO Hot Work Class I within 11m radius of paint mixing/application area.
Permit
Paint waste: dispose in owner-provided containers — NEVER pour on floor or into drains.
Banned
📦 Material
Fire extinguisher: Fire Rating ≥6A 20B, size ≥20 lbs — 1 per work point.
Required

📋 Permit to Work (PTW) Procedure

Full lifecycle permit management at LSP — from package preparation through on-site approval, active work monitoring, and formal closure. Covering all high-risk work types.

Critical Rule: The Permit Requester and Approver CANNOT be the same person in the same permit.
⚠️
Important: Contractors CANNOT act as Permit Requester at LSP. Critical Work requires a dedicated Contractor Safety (1:1 per job).
⏱️
"1-Hour Rule": Work must start within 1 hour of PTW approval — otherwise Re-approval is required.
👥 Roles & Responsibilities

HSSE Manager

  • Establish PTW training program & competency standards
  • Supervise & audit PTW system
📋
PTW Document Approval Team
  • Final approval of PTW
  • Review PTW + certificates before signing
  • Verify all safety conditions are in place
  • Right to reject if requirements not met
🔍
PTW On-site Approval Team
  • Field safety condition verification
  • Attend TBM, JSA/JICA talk
  • Monitor working conditions continuously
  • Stop work if unsafe conditions arise
📝
Permit Requester (LSP Staff)
  • Prepare full PTW Package (WMS + JSA/JICA)
  • Submit to Area Owner on time
  • Ensure contractor compliance
  • Cannot be same as Approver
🦺
Contractor
  • Deploy Contractor Safety (1:1 for Critical Work)
  • Non-critical: 1 Safety → max 3 jobs within 18m
  • Fire Watcher (Hot Work); Hole Watchman (CSE)
  • Daily health check for CSE & working at height >12m
⚡
Authorized Employee
  • Trained/authorized to implement safe practices
  • Authority to sign, audit, maintain monitoring equipment
  • Must be "competent" per required training
🔄 PTW Lifecycle — 7 Phases
A
Phase A — Prepare PTW Package
§5.2.1 – 5.2.3 · WMS + JSA/JICA + Supporting Docs
▼
1
Determine if work requires PTW (hot work, CSE, excavation, electrical, lifting…) — check mandatory work list §5.2.1.
2
Organize meeting to prepare WMS + JSA/JICA and control measures for all potential hazards.
3
Prepare PTW Package: WMS · JSA/JICA · Personnel/equipment list · Plot plan · Certificates (Lifting plan, Excavation, CSE, Rescue plan, Ventilation plan…).
4
Section/Dept Manager (Requester side) signs Section IV of PTW cover page to confirm review and approval for Requester to control activities.
B
Phase B — Submit to Area Owner
§5.2.4 · Submit 1 day in advance
▼
1
PTW Package must be submitted to Area Owner 1 day before scheduled work; urgent cases require Area Owner agreement.
2
Place PTW Package in correct PTW box (Area Owner / Submitted) at CCB (both Upstream & Downstream) to prevent loss/confusion.
3
All supporting docs (WMS, JSA, certificates) must be approved and registered in Gate Keeper meeting 1 week in advance (per WMS workflow HS-D-2000-016).
C
Phase C — Document Approval
§5.3 · Non-critical / Critical / Holiday approval
▼
📌 Non-Critical Work
Approved by Section Manager
🔶 Critical Work
Approved by Shift Supervisor (US only) & Department Manager
🔴 Hot Work Class I & CSE — Holiday/Weekend
Final approver: Manufacturing Director US/DS
🌙 Night Extension (6PM–6AM) — Hot Work I & CSE
Final approver: Manufacturing Director US/DS
1
Approver signs PTW & related documents · Assigns PTW number on cover page · Updates PTW log sheet.
2
Online approval: Requester scans/photographs and sends via email/Line/Zalo; Approver replies "Approved/Rejected PTW No.…"; Requester prints and attaches as evidence.
D
Phase D — On-site Approval + TBM/KYT
§5.4 · Field verification + Toolbox Talk
▼
1
Receive approved PTW & bring to site; apply mitigation measures per JSA.
2
On-site approval team inspects & confirms: safety conditions on site + signs JSA form HS-F-2000-009.
3
Conduct JSA + Toolbox Talk + KYT — work team understands and commits to steps, hazards, and controls.
4
Ensure permit is posted/available at the work location.
⏱️
1-Hour Rule: Work must start as soon as possible after approval. PTW becomes invalid if work does not begin within 1 hour — Re-approval required.
E
Phase E — Active Work Monitoring
§5.5–5.6 · Continuous surveillance
▼
1
Requester notifies Permit Approver or Board Man of affected unit at start time; Approver/Board Man notifies related units.
2
Continuous monitoring of safety measures under control of on-site approval team.
3
HSSE/Task force audits PTW during work; violations recorded and sanctions applied per severity level.
4
If conditions change → update WMS + JSA/JICA → re-approved by Authorized Person before continuing.
5
Daily health check (CSE/working on temporary platform >12m): alcohol · blood pressure · temperature recorded in HS-F-2000-013.
F
Phase F — Stop Work / Re-approval / Cancel
§5.6 · Triggers and conditions
▼
🔁 Re-approval Required When:
  • Work not started within 1 hour of approval
  • Team member changes (Requester / Contractor Safety / Worker)
  • Stopped due to unsafe conditions that cannot be immediately fixed
  • Symptoms arise, JSA/JICA non-compliance, or risks outside JSA/JICA scope
❌ PTW Cancelled When:
  • Plant-wide emergency alarm
  • Sudden unsafe condition change
  • Observed non-compliance with permit requirements
G
Phase G — Close & Archive Permit
§5.8 · Site cleanup + signatures + record keeping
▼
1
Clean up site · Completion check · 5S check · Power off/shut down related equipment.
2
Sign RTO form (HS-F-2000-010) and sign permit closure on-site.
3
Collect all required signatures: Contractor Safety · Requester · Field Operator.
4
Update PTW log sheet · Return Original to HSSE · Verify document completeness.
5
Review PTW performance & feedback to relevant parties (if applicable) · Archive records.

⚙️ Process Safety Management (PSM)

Application of management systems and controls to identify, understand, and control process hazards — preventing catastrophic incidents and protecting people, assets, and the environment.

14

❓ Why PSM is Needed
📋
Outdated Procedures
Operating/emergency procedures insufficient or not updated in time.
🔧
Non-conforming Equipment
Equipment changes not approved or designed to incorrect standards.
🎓
Insufficient Training
Inadequate job competency, poor supervision, lack of process technology understanding.
🔍
Poor Maintenance
Inadequate inspection/maintenance programs leading to unexpected equipment failure.
🏛️ Three Pillars & 14 PSM Elements
⚙️ PILLAR 1 — TECHNOLOGY
1
Process Safety Information (PSI)
Foundation for identifying & understanding hazards. Covers material hazards, process design basis, and equipment design basis. Must be documented and updated periodically.
2
Process Hazards Analysis (PHA)
Combines science, skill, and judgment to identify, evaluate, and control significant hazards. Basis for tracking recommendations and communicating to stakeholders.
3
Operating Procedures & Safe Practices (SOP/SWP)
SOP: clear operating procedures — startup, normal, temporary, emergency, shutdown. SWP: permit system for non-routine work (LOTO, hot work, confined space…).
4
Management of Technology Change (MOC-T)
System to analyze & review technology changes, identify potential hazards, and ensure training is updated before changes take effect.
🏗️ PILLAR 2 — FACILITIES
5
Quality Assurance (QA)
Ensures equipment, materials, and services meet appropriate technical standards. Covers quality control from design, procurement, construction to maintenance.
6
Pre-Startup Safety Review (PSSR)
Ensures all management systems are ready before introducing material into a process. Applied for new facilities or after significant modifications. Performed by multi-functional team.
7
Mechanical Integrity (MI)
Ensures mechanical integrity of pressure vessels, relief systems, piping… through periodic inspection, testing, and maintenance with full documentation.
8
Management of Change – Facilities (MOC-F)
Manages subtle facility changes — seemingly minor modifications that may impact safety if not properly controlled.
👷 PILLAR 3 — PERSONNEL
9
Training and Performance
70:20:10 model (practice/mentoring/classroom). Refresher training max every 3 years. Personnel must meet "Fit for duty" standard.
10
Contractor Safety Management (CSM)
Clear responsibility for selecting, verifying, and managing contractors/partners to ensure consistency and eliminate/prevent contractor-related incidents.
11
Incident Investigation (II)
Actively and thoroughly investigate all serious incidents via a 13-step process — from initial report to CA/PA recommendations and record keeping.
12
Management of Personnel Change (MOC-P)
Applied for promotions/transfers, resignations, illness/death, workforce reductions. Ensures minimum required competency/experience level is maintained.
13
Emergency Planning & Response (EPR)
In-depth planning: risk analysis, response strategies, EPR plan, training & drills. Coordination between plant and community.
14
Auditing
Periodic audits to verify compliance and implement corrective actions. Identifies system weaknesses and continuously improves safety performance.
